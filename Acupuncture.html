Acupuncture Techniques

JapaneseAcupuncture


Japanese acupuncture is a style of acupuncture that originated in classical Chinese medicine and was introduced to Japan in the 6th century. It's known for its gentle, subtle approach, and uses finer needles that are inserted more shallowly than other styles of acupuncture. Japanese acupuncture also emphasizes palpation, or touch, before needle treatment, often focusing on the abdominal area and meridians.



Moxibustion


Moxibustion is a traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) treatment that involves burning moxa, an herb, on or above the skin to stimulate acupuncture points or affected areas. The heat from the moxa can dilate blood vessels, promote blood flow, and help with muscle relaxation. Moxibustion can be used as a stand-alone therapy or in combination with acupuncture.
